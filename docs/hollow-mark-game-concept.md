# Hollow Mark Game Concept

Working codename: **Hollow Mark**.

This is not a small side game and not a streamer reward widget. It is a persistent world-game that can start as a web app, grow into a full-stack live universe, and later become a richer desktop-grade client.

The stream can open a window into the world, but the world must survive without the stream.

## Core Promise

Players enter a deep, intimate, dangerous-looking universe and become a **Mask**: not a class, not a faction, not a carnival role, but a living identity that changes through choices.

Every meaningful action leaves a trace in the world. Some traces are private. Some become visible marks. Some change rooms for everyone. Players return because the place remembers what happened, not because a daily chore told them to come back.

The feeling should be:

- clear enough to play immediately
- deep enough to obsess over
- tense without becoming boringly cryptic
- beautiful and unsettling without becoming theatrical, royal, religious, or medieval
- tied to the jester as intelligence, contradiction, elegance, and danger, not circus or stage
- tied to dragon-pride as inner force and pressure, not a literal monster on every screen

## The Game Loop

1. A player enters the scene and creates a Mask.
2. The Mask receives a drive: Softness, Defiance, Pride, or Static.
3. The player chooses a zone in the living map.
4. The player spends scarce will on one strong move.
5. The move changes personal state, public room state, or both.
6. Other players see consequences in the map and react with their own moves.
7. Over time, visible marks, unstable rooms, rival traces, and rare openings form a persistent history.

The important part: the player is always playing. They are not only watching lore, waiting for a stream event, solving disposable riddles, or collecting routine rewards.

## First Playable Shape

The MVP should be a browser game with a premium animated entrance scene and a persistent map behind it.

First playable systems:

- Mask creation with one chosen drive.
- A small world map with 5-7 zones.
- A limited daily or session-based action budget, but never a chore checklist.
- Moves that change the world state.
- A visible trace history per zone.
- Marks that appear as consequences, not generic badges.
- A public world pulse that shows tension, clarity, and fracture.
- A creator/admin view that can observe the world, not command cheap stream punishments.

The player should never feel: "I click a button for points."  
They should feel: "I changed something, and now everyone enters a slightly different place."

## Reference Mix From Drako Lair

The provided Drako Lair reference is now an authorized inspiration source, but not a visual replacement.

Keep and evolve:

- **Map presence:** regions/zones as places that can change.
- **Reveal pressure:** hidden states become visible through player action.
- **Collection feeling:** players keep visible consequences, marks, scars, and traces.
- **Chronicle memory:** the world records important changes as history.
- **Admin control:** creator tools matter, especially safety, cost governance, and manual curation.
- **Kick bridge:** stream can observe or expose world state, but cannot become the core loop.

Change deeply:

- Daily offering becomes scarce will and meaningful moves.
- Fog reveal becomes pressure, clarity, fracture, and trace visibility.
- Cards become marks/scars/traces unless a later card-like system earns its place.
- Chronicle becomes an action history with consequences, not only a feed.
- Overlay becomes a world pulse window, not a streamer gimmick surface.

The current 2.5D jester scene remains the art direction: deep violet, orange and pistachio tension, black-glass material, arc camera, grounded character, no carnival, no stage, no sacred or monarchic language.

## Why It Can Grow

Hollow Mark can become ambitious because the core is not a single mini-game. It is a world-state engine.

Growth paths:

- **Social pressure:** players can protect, expose, bind, sever, or distort traces left by others.
- **Hidden information:** not everything is visible, but every hidden thing must have readable symptoms.
- **Persistent territories:** zones become warmer, clearer, locked, corrupted, or claimed by repeated action.
- **Seasonless evolution:** the map mutates from accumulated player behavior instead of reset-based events.
- **Stream relation:** a live stream can reveal the world pulse or unlock a rare window, but the stream is not the main gameplay.
- **Desktop future:** richer camera, layered audio, heavier visual effects, and deeper realtime presence can be added later without changing the core rules.

## Mystery Without Boredom

Mystery is only useful when it creates hunger.

Rules for keeping it playable:

- Every screen must show what the player can do now.
- Every move must preview its risk and likely consequence.
- Every result must show what changed.
- Hidden systems must leave visible symptoms.
- Lore must be earned through play, not dumped as text.
- Rare events must come from world pressure, not random streamer gimmicks.
- The player must never need to guess whether the app is broken, empty, or waiting for a stream.

## Zone Loom

The world map should not start as a flat map screen. The first version is the **Zone Loom**: a diegetic pressure projection inside the current room.

Each zone exposes:

- pressure
- clarity
- fracture
- visible trace count
- state: veiled, listening, pressured, fractured, opened
- intensity

Selecting a zone should alter the scene before a player commits a move. A move then makes that same zone pressure stronger, not as a popup reward, but as a material change in the room.

This keeps the map feeling like the place is becoming readable, instead of turning the product into a normal quest board.

## Move Forecast

Mystery must never hide the basic consequence of pressing a button. Before a player commits a move, Hollow Mark should forecast:

- expected pressure change
- expected clarity change
- fracture risk
- whether the trace will likely be buried, veiled, or visible
- the selected zone's likely next state

The forecast should be felt first in the room material: zone pressure lines, floor tension, altered reflection, or subtle Mask resonance. The UI may summarize it, but the scene must carry the weight.

This is how the app stays readable without becoming a standard tutorial panel, quest board, or streamer action menu.

## Player Identity

A Mask is built from tension, not from a standard RPG class.

Initial drives:

- **Softness:** protection, intimacy, refusal to harden completely.
- **Defiance:** pressure, refusal to obey the room's expected path.
- **Pride:** controlled dominance, elegance, and dangerous self-possession.
- **Static:** interference, wrong signals, hidden resistance, and strange timing.

These drives affect how moves behave. They should not lock players into obvious factions.

The Mask must also have a visible evolving shape. It starts from the chosen drive, then changes through decisions, scars, visible marks, world pressure, and accidents. The player should be able to say: "my Mask looks like this because of what happened to me here."

Early shape variables:

- **silhouette:** veiled, lifted, offset, forward-leaning, split-crest
- **surface:** soft-glass, ember-cut, pistachio-lacquer, interference, scarred black-glass
- **facets:** softness, defiance, pride, static
- **fracture:** accumulated cost of risky choices
- **visibility:** how much of the Mask has become public through traces

## Moves

The first move set should be small and heavy:

- **Mark:** leave a visible consequence in a zone.
- **Veil:** hide a trace while increasing pressure.
- **Bind:** connect two traces so later actions echo between them.
- **Sever:** break a connection and create a scar.
- **Expose:** reveal hidden pressure or a buried trace.
- **Bend:** redirect a zone's tension toward another zone.
- **Spare:** protect a zone from overload, at personal cost.

No move should exist just to fill a menu. Every move should change the world, the Mask, or both.

## Visible Rewards

Visible marks are allowed. The issue is not the existence of rewards; the issue is generic reward language.

Allowed:

- scars, signatures, altered reflections, marks on the Mask, changed room geometry, rare visible states
- status that has meaning inside the world
- recognition that comes from irreversible or risky choices

Avoid:

- streak farming
- generic points
- streamer challenge tokens
- flat achievement stickers
- routine missions

## Full-Stack Direction

Backend entities:

- users
- masks
- zones
- moves
- traces
- marks
- world state snapshots
- public pulse snapshots
- moderation/admin actions

Realtime layer:

- world pulse updates
- zone pressure changes
- new visible traces
- creator observer view

Storage principle:

- Keep an append-only action log.
- Derive visible world state from actions.
- Store snapshots for performance, but keep the history meaningful.

## Product North Star

Hollow Mark should feel like entering a place that is already watching, already remembering, and already changing because people dared to touch it.

It should be playable on its own, streamable when wanted, and ambitious enough that it can become the main project rather than a decoration around streaming.
